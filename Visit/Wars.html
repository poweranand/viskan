Kanchipuram has many houses that are more than a century old. This is not an uncommon fact for a city that is live for more than thousand years. Out of the many heritage houses two need to mentioned for two reasons- 1. The age of the house 2. Their access to visitors/tourists. They are-
The Shakunthala Jagannathan Museum of Folk Art- This 400 year old house belonging to the land owners of Damal, a village near by Kanchipuram is situated in the Lingappan Street near the Ekambaranathar Temple. Reliable sources date it to the Vijayanagara period-15-16th centuries. The existence of wall paintings, sculptures and household collections prompted the reuse of the house as a museum.
This house is maintained by Sir C.P. Ramaswami Aiyar Foundation. This house is open to visitors daily from 09.30 am to 05.00 pm. The house will remain closed for 15 days during the Panguni Uthiram festival. The entrance fee is Rs.20/-
Kanchi Kudil-This is a 90 year old house, renovated but authentic in its old style architecture and elegant functionality. Kanchi Kudil provides an opportunity to tourists to get a feel of how the local people lived in Kanchipuram. The simple furniture, the daily ise articles, the master’s room, the ladies room, the children room, the pooja room, the kitchen, the courtyards, the backyard with agricultural implements are all kept open with suitable exhibits so that a visitor can get a glimpse of how an agricultural family lived in Kanchipuram not so long ago.
Kanchi Kudil is located in Sangeetha Vidwan Nayanar Pillai Street(Old Putheri Street) near the Kachapeswarar Temple.
