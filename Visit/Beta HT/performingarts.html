Kanchipuram is famous for folk art performances. Theru Koothu is conducted in the nights during the Amman temple festivals in the surrounding villages. Theru Koothu is an open-air, folk drama form having dialogue, song and dance components. Usually epic related story themes are chosen for play. The artistes mostly belong to rural peasant families. The audiences are also villagers who will sit all night enjoying the high pitch songs of the artistes. A teacher well versed in this art will train his group before every performance.
Every year, during the mid or end of May festivities will start in the Draupathi Amman temples in the northern part of Tamil Nadu. In Kanchipuram and surrounding areas Mahabharatham discourse will be done in the day time. In a grand Pandal, one story-teller will sit and tell the stories along with some musical accompaniments. The people will hear him large numbers. Mahabharatham story will be told in the day time; Koothu will be performed in the night time.
